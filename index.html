System Overview

  • A diagram of the water system is included in the cabin instructions manual.
  • The dam, with two large round water tanks and one small rectangular tank, is upstream (access via the path next to the log seat on the beach).

Drinking Water

  • The small rectangular tank connects only to the kitchen cold tap.
  • This water is not treated, so either:
    • Boil and cool it before drinking, or
    • Bring your own drinking water

Hot Water & Gas

  • Boiler: In the bathroom ‚Äì Instructions on boiler.
  • Showers: Set to double flame symbol and level 2.
  • Kitchen hot water: Use triple flame symbol and level 3 or 4 if cold.
  • Gas Taps: Taps are off when turned across the pipe, and on when turned along the pipe.

Gas Bottles

  • Located outside in the cupboard under the porch window.
  • Two bottles connected with an auto-changeover valve.
  • If gas seems low, try shaking the bottles before swapping.
  • Spare bottle: Chained under the deck. Key in drawer beneath bread bin.
  • Return empty bottle under the deck and re-chain.

Septic Tank

  • Flush ONLY toilet roll.
  • Use Ecover toilet cleaner only.

Troubleshooting

  • If the kitchen cold tap runs brown, slows, or stops:
    • Check the filter under the cabin (beneath the kitchen window):
      • It‚Äôs a clear plastic cylinder with a string filter inside.
      • Access from outside using the spanner handle labelled and hanging in the porch.
      • Spare string filters are in the kitchen sink unit.
      • Leave dirty filters out ‚Äì they are taken away to be cleaned.
    • Check the dam intake filter:
      • Located inside the bottom of the dam ‚Äî may be blocked.
      • Lower the dam‚Äôs water level.
      • Turn off the tank outlet.
      • Open the dam drain valve.
    • Tools required (all in the bathroom tool drawer):
      • Valve tops
      • Adjustable spanner
      • Screwdriver
    • Note: The flow valve between tanks is a screwhead on the pipe ‚Äî inline = open.
    • Drain and clean the tanks ‚Äî they collect a peaty residue and may need occasional cleaning.

Winter Use (November ‚Äì March)

  • The water system is turned off and drained in winter.
  • Before turning on, ensure:
    • All taps inside the cabin are closed.
    • The drain point on the boiler is screwed back in (thin brass vertical fitting).
  • To turn water back on:
    • Locate the stop tap under the cabin (beneath the kitchen sink).
    • Valves to turn on:
      • Tap heads for all valves are in the tool drawer in the porch.
      • Outlet from the tanks (by the dam)
      • Valve under the kitchen window (beneath the back of the cabin)
      • Pipe to kitchen cold tap (has the clear filter bottle)

Battery System

  • Batteries are located in the tall cupboard in the porch.
  • They charge from the solar panel (always active) and the wind turbine.
  • There is a brake switch to protect the turbine‚Äôs bearings in high wind:
    • On arrival: Turn the brake off so the turbine can charge.
    • During gales: Use the brake.
    • Before leaving: Ensure the brake is on.

Isolator Switch

  • Red switch on the outside of the battery cupboard.
  • Turn ON (clockwise) when you arrive to power lights and USB ports.
  • Turn OFF (anticlockwise) when leaving.

Inverter & Sockets

  • To use: Flip the red lever on the circuit breaker to the horizontal position.
  • Then switch ON the plug socket switch ‚Äî lights will come on.
  • When done, switch OFF the inverter and press the red button to reset the circuit breaker (lever pops up at 45¬∞).
  • Please only use the inverter when the battery is well charged ‚Äî it can drain power quickly!

Inverter controls: breaker + switch

Low-Power Charging Option

  • A 12V car-style socket by the end of the sofa bed is suitable for phone chargers and uses less power than the inverter.

Gaslights & Lighting

  • Be extremely careful not to touch the mantles ‚Äî they are fragile and hard to replace.
  • Use a taper or long match (in kitchen drawer) to light.

USB & Solar Lights

  • The little USB light above the table is rechargeable and portable ‚Äî great light quality.

Fitting a New Gas Bottle

  • Push the connector down until you hear a very faint click.
  • Test you've pushed hard enough by pulling upwards ‚Äì it shouldn't come off or wobble.
  • Turn the black tap clockwise until it locks the lever down ‚Äì this means gas is flowing.
  • To remove the connector, reverse the process: turn the tap anticlockwise and press in.

Main Supply Tap

  • The yellow tap between the tanks turns gas on/off for the whole cabin.
  • If no gas is working inside, this is the most likely cause.

Changeover Regulator

  • The black lever should point to the primary bottle (the one currently in use).
  • There is a small indicator window that changes colour to show when the primary bottle is empty.
  • Indicator states:
    • Clear: Both bottles are full.
    • Red: Primary bottle is empty ‚Äì time to swap.
  • If the indicator is red, replace the bottle the black lever is pointing to.
  • After connecting a new bottle, manually switch the black lever to the opposite bottle ‚Äì the window should return to Clear.

Clear = Full

Red = Empty

üö§ Boats & Engines

‚ñ∂ View Details

Tide Caution

  • During high tides (especially at night), always pull the dinghies up to the grass and tie them to both the trailer and the tree.

Safety Equipment

  • Always carry a bailer and sponge in each dinghy.
  • Adult and children‚Äôs life jackets are stored on the rack in the porch.

Tioram (Aluminium Rowboat)

  • Oars with captive rowlocks are in the shed.
  • Fit rowlocks by pointing the oar forward so the lug on the rowlock aligns with the groove in the slot ‚Äî they are fragile ‚Äî tie them with cord to avoid losing them overboard.
  • Ensure the white plastic drain bung (rear of the boat) is screwed in or the boat will fill with water.
  • Slatted floorboards (three for Tioram) are in the shed or boats ‚Äî place inside when using for a flat floor and hull protection.

Shona (Wooden Dinghy)

  • Rowlocks hang inside the cabin, named on the side.
  • Long wooden oars are nearby.
  • Spare oars and rowlocks are in the shed.

Outboard Engines

Large Tohatsu (Unleaded Petrol)

  • Uses standard unleaded petrol.
  • Most powerful engine ‚Äî best suited for Shona and Tioram.
  • Always take spare petrol with you.

Small Tohatsu (Petrol + Oil Mix)

  • Requires 50:1 fuel mix ‚Äî 50 parts petrol to 1 part oil.
  • Oil is stored in the shed alongside the engine.
  • Less powerful ‚Äî suitable for inflatables and plastic boat.
  • Consider taking this engine as a spare for longer trips.

Engine Use & Safety

  • Tie the engine onto the boat using a cord to prevent it being dropped overboard.
  • Always use the red emergency stop spiral line, clipped to the driver.

Cooling System Check

  • When running, there should be a steady stream of seawater from the coolant outlet (located above water level on the shaft).
  • If the stream is intermittent or absent, the intake may be blocked (e.g., with seaweed).
  • Use the piece of wire attached to the large Tohatsu to clear the intake (a rubber tube inside the propeller shaft).

Use with Shona (Wooden Boat)

  • Place the plywood boards marked ‚ÄúShona‚Äù on either side of the boat‚Äôs transom (rear) to prevent the outboard fixings from damaging the wood.

After Use Maintenance

  • Run the outboard through fresh water (using the tank and hose) to flush out salt.
  • While flushing, switch off the fuel supply to allow the carburettor to drain.

‚úÖ Cabin Departure Checklist

‚ñ∂ View Details

To help keep the cabin stocked and functional for everyone, please run through the following before you leave:

Bedding

  • Strip used bedding.
  • Stack mattresses upright and apart to air.
  • Stack duvets, pillows, etc. over the beams as found.
  • Close all upper windows.

Kitchen

  • Turn off the fridge tap (on the line under the hob).
  • Clean out the fridge and leave the door vent open to prevent mould.
  • Empty all rubbish:
    • Compost ‚Üí heap behind the rock near the fuel shed.
    • Burnable paper/card ‚Üí burn on the beach below the high tide line.
    • Everything else ‚Üí take away with you (mice use rubbish for bedding).
  • Store boxes of tissues in the bathroom cupboard (to deter mice).
  • Do not leave perishables out. Only sealed, unopened food may stay in cupboards.
  • Clean out the stove and empty the ash tray onto the compost heap.
  • Refill logs, kindling, and smokeless fuel for the next visitors.
  • Please leave the kitchen and cabin clean and tidy ‚Äî as you'd like to find it.

Porch & Bathroom

  • Turn off the boiler at the control knob.
  • In the gas cupboard, turn the valve to disconnect the gas bottles.
  • (Winter only): Drain the boiler using the labelled drain point ‚Äî use a bucket.
  • In the tall battery cupboard:
    • Switch on the brake for the wind generator (labelled panel above batteries).
    • Turn the isolator switch on the outside of the cupboard to OFF.
  • Lock and bolt the back door, and shut the window.
  • Hang the shower curtain over the rail.
  • Lock the porch door from inside the cabin, and leave the key in the drawer.

Boats & Outboard

  • Ensure all boats are padlocked.
  • Tioram:
    • Pull up under the deck, bung out, and rinse with fresh water.
    • Run cable and padlock through the trolley, loop around one of the bench seats inside.
    • Prop the trolley so the weight is off the tyre and angled to drain via the bung.
  • If an outboard was used, run it through with fresh water using the hose to clear salt from the system.

Quad Bike

  • Rinse the quad (and trailer, if used) with the hose.
  • Apply WD40 to working parts underneath.
  • Lock using the cable around the axle and pillar.
  • Replace the cover and straps as found.

üîê Final Checks

  • Lock the shed.
  • Clean, lock, and cover the quad.
  • Drop the latch on the main door and use the deadlock.
